# Objection Handling

## Overview

Drafts responses to common objections while staying human and non-pushy.

## Principles
1. **Validate and Empathize:** Always acknowledge the concern. Never argue.
2. **Surface Core Tension:** Is it timing? Security? Budget?
3. **Low-friction pivot:** Propose a smaller value-add (article, case study, later follow-up) instead of forcing a meeting.

## Procedure

1. Identify the objection (timing, competitor, security, cost).
2. Acknowledge it plainly.
3. Respond with one relevant proof or clarification.
4. Offer a low-friction next step or close politely.

## Common Objection Angles
- **"Not now":** Respect the calendar. Suggest a 3-6 month check-in.
- **"Already have a partner":** Validate the relationship, offer to be a "second pair of eyes" for specific high-performance or security-heavy use cases.
- **"Too expensive":** Pivot to ROI/Pilot value.
- **"Security/Privacy":** Provide enterprise-grade documentation link.

## Verification Checklist

- [ ] No pressure/robot-flattery.
- [ ] No unsupported claims.
- [ ] Explicit next action (follow-up date, or polite closing).
- [ ] Respects opt-outs/timing.
